# SimTower Magic - What Made It Special

## üéØ Core Magic (from research)

### 1. PERSONAL SCALE
- Follow INDIVIDUAL people through their day
- Name and color-code specific tenants
- Feel loss when someone moves out
- Watch reviewers tour your hotel
- Observe maids cleaning rooms

### 2. INTIMATE PROBLEMS (not abstract stats)
- "My neighbors are too loud"
- "The elevator is too slow"
- "My parking spot is inconvenient"
- Noise complaints between floor types
- Individual satisfaction, not aggregate

### 3. DAY/NIGHT RHYTHM
- Sun rises ‚Üí businesses open
- Sun sets ‚Üí everyone goes home
- Rush hour elevator chaos
- Night shift workers vs day workers
- Hotels busiest at night, offices by day

### 4. ELEVATOR OBSESSION
- The HEART of the game
- Express vs local elevators
- Sky lobbies for transfers
- Watching stress levels
- Optimizing routes like a puzzle

### 5. PROGRESSIVE UNLOCKS
- Start with basics (lobby, office, stairs)
- Star rating unlocks new buildings
- Cathedral at floor 100 = victory
- Each star feels like an achievement

### 6. CLICK-AND-DRAG SIMPLICITY
- Click ‚Üí drag ‚Üí release = building placed
- No complex menus mid-flow
- Instant visual feedback
- Satisfying "snap" to grid

## ‚ùå What's Wrong with Current OpenTower

### Controls
- [ ] Pan/zoom feels janky or broken
- [ ] Building placement unclear
- [ ] No clear "how do I start?" guidance
- [ ] Mouse interactions don't feel natural

### Visuals
- [ ] Colored rectangles, not charming pixel art
- [ ] No personality in buildings
- [ ] Missing the cozy 90s aesthetic
- [ ] People aren't visible/cute

### Game Feel
- [ ] No satisfying feedback on actions
- [ ] Missing "juice" (sounds, particles)
- [ ] Day/night not visually dramatic
- [ ] No sense of a living building

### Onboarding
- [ ] Player doesn't know what to do
- [ ] No tutorial or hints
- [ ] Building menu is confusing
- [ ] No clear first goal

## ‚úÖ Priority Fixes

### P0: MAKE IT PLAYABLE (Critical)
1. **Fix camera controls** - WASD or edge pan + scroll zoom
2. **Fix building placement** - Click menu, click-drag on grid
3. **Add first-run tutorial** - "Build a lobby, then offices"
4. **Show money and goals clearly**

### P1: MAKE IT LOOK GOOD
1. **Pixel art buildings** - Replace rectangles with sprites
2. **Cute tiny people** - 4-8px characters that walk around
3. **Day/night lighting** - Dramatic, cozy, atmospheric
4. **Animated elements** - Elevators move, lights flicker

### P2: MAKE IT FEEL GOOD
1. **Sound effects** - Build sounds, ambient noise
2. **Particle effects** - Construction dust, money floating
3. **Screen shake** - Subtle on big events
4. **Notifications that delight** - Not just info dumps

### P3: MAKE IT DEEP
1. **Elevator management UI** - Visual, intuitive
2. **Tenant details** - Click to see their story
3. **Financial reports** - Satisfying charts
4. **Events and disasters** - Keep it interesting

## üé® Visual Reference

### Original SimTower Look:
- 16-color palette feel (even with more colors)
- Tiny detailed pixel art
- Cross-section view (see inside rooms)
- People as 4-8px sprites
- Warm, inviting colors
- Grid is subtle, not harsh

### Modern pixel art that nails it:
- Project Highrise (spiritual successor)
- Tiny Tower (mobile)
- Kairosoft games (pixel management sims)
